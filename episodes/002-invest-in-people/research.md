# Research Brief: "Investing in Humans" — Land the Plane (ep 002)

Scope note: All claims sourced. Where a stat rests on shaky or secondhand
methodology, it's flagged. Two primary sources (the *Journal of Management*
loneliness review and the Gallup 2026 report PDF) blocked direct fetch
(HTTP 403); their figures are corroborated across two independent search
results each and flagged accordingly.

## A. Current state (Feb–May 2026)

**Gallup, *State of the Global Workplace 2026* (released Apr 2026).** Global
employee engagement fell to **20% in 2025**, lowest since 2020; Gallup pegs
lost productivity at roughly **$10 trillion** (a modeled estimate, treat as
directional, not precise). **More than 1 in 5 employees report loneliness**;
for workers under 35 it climbs to **~26%**. A striking inversion: **leaders
report the highest engagement (26%) and thriving (43%) but also the highest
loneliness (31%)** — being central doesn't protect you.
[Gallup](https://www.gallup.com/workplace/349484/state-of-the-global-workplace.aspx) ·
[FlexOS summary](https://www.flexos.work/learn/gallups-employee-engagement-2026) ·
[Haiilo](https://blog.haiilo.com/blog/2026-gallup-state-of-the-global-workplace-report/)

**New academic review (2026):** McCarthy, Erdogan, Bauer, Kudret & Campion,
*"All the Lonely People,"* **Journal of Management (2026)**, synthesizes
**233 empirical studies**. Core distinction: **isolation is objective (being
alone); loneliness is the subjective sense that your relationships are
deficient — so you can be deeply lonely in a crowded office (or a busy
Slack).**
[SAGE/Journal of Management](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/01492063241313320) ·
[PMC mirror](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12701760/)

**Amy Edmondson on AI + psychological safety (2025–26).** Her current
framing: AI is "a two-edged sword — only in environments where people feel
safe do they speak openly, experiment, and view AI as an opportunity rather
than a threat." The live question: *will people feel safe admitting they
don't understand an AI-generated recommendation?* Direct bridge to catching
bad agent output.
[UNSW BusinessThink interview](https://www.businessthink.unsw.edu.au/articles/psychological-safety-amy-edmondson-remote-work-artificial-intelligence) ·
[Psychology Today, Sep 2025](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/decisions-decisions/202509/psychological-safety-drives-ai-adoption)

**Anthropic, *2026 Agentic Coding Trends Report*.** Counter-data: developers
use AI in **~60% of work but fully delegate only 0–20% of tasks** — the
harder/more design-dependent the task, the more they keep it human or
collaborative. The report frames the engineer's job as shifting to
"orchestrating agents... while maintaining the human judgment, oversight, and
collaboration that ensures quality."
[Anthropic report](https://resources.anthropic.com/2026-agentic-coding-trends-report) ·
[PDF](https://resources.anthropic.com/hubfs/2026%20Agentic%20Coding%20Trends%20Report.pdf)

**Counterpoints / skepticism to flag on air:**
- The Anthropic data argues *against* the simple "AI isolates engineers"
  narrative — humans stay in the loop on hard work. Honest move: concede the
  tool doesn't force isolation; the *temptation* and the option do.
- Gallup's "best friend at work" item is the most contested item in the Q12
  battery; managers and academics have long pushed back that it's awkward and
  proxies for other engagement drivers.
  [Sicora Consulting critique](https://sicoraconsulting.com/leadertips/pushing-back-on-the-gallupq12/)
- The "$10T" and the often-cited "$154B annual cost of workplace loneliness"
  are modeled/extrapolated figures — cite as order-of-magnitude, not gospel.

## B. Deeper-context concepts & quotes

- **Surgeon General's Advisory, *Our Epidemic of Loneliness and Isolation*
  (May 2, 2023, 81 pp).** Anchor stat: social disconnection carries mortality
  risk **comparable to smoking up to 15 cigarettes a day**, with **+29% heart
  disease, +32% stroke** risk. Explicitly names workplaces as a domain where
  "performance, productivity, and engagement are diminished."
  [HHS/NCBI full text](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK595227/) ·
  [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2023/05/02/1173418268/loneliness-connection-mental-health-dementia-surgeon-general)

- **Google's Project Aristotle (Rozovsky, ~2012–15).** Studied **180 teams /
  250 attributes over 2 years**; **psychological safety** was the #1
  differentiator. Verbatim (NYT, Duhigg, 2016): *"What Project Aristotle has
  taught people within Google is that no one wants to put on a 'work face'
  when they get to the office."*
  [re:Work / Psych Safety summary](https://psychsafety.com/googles-project-aristotle/)

- **Patrick Lencioni, *Five Dysfunctions of a Team* — vulnerability-based
  trust** sits at the base of the pyramid: trust is *"a willingness to be
  completely vulnerable with one another — to let down our guard, admit our
  flaws, and ask for help."* That last clause — *ask for help* — is the muscle
  that atrophies when you can ask an agent instead of a colleague.
  [Five Behaviors](https://www.fivebehaviors.com/blog/establishing-a-foundation-of-trust/)

- **Stephen M.R. Covey, *The Speed of Trust*.** *"When trust goes down, speed
  will also go down and costs will go up. When trust goes up, speed will also
  go up and costs will go down."* The agentic irony: agents make *typing*
  fast; trust still governs the speed of everything between people.
  [Speed of Trust](https://speedoftrust.com/)

- **Rest taxonomy / recovery science.** APA frames **social rest** as distinct
  from physical/mental rest — restoration *with* supportive others.
  [APA, seven rest types](https://www.apa.org/topics/mental-health/seven-rest-types)
  Recovery research adds: the recovery-driving ingredients are **psychological
  detachment + positive emotion**; social breaks let you "feel part of a
  group," but detaching from job demands is what restores. *Social rest works
  only when you're not still working through someone.*
  [Break review (ResearchGate)](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/361913348_Role_of_work_breaks_in_well-being_and_performance_A_systematic_review_and_future_research_agenda) ·
  [detachment study, PMC](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7596587/)

- **Sabbatical evidence (SHRM lineage):** extended leave lowered stress and
  burnout, and the effect **persisted after return**; the better-resting
  cohort rested *proactively, before* being depleted, and in
  **emotional/relational** modes — not just sleep.
  [SHRM](https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/news/benefits-compensation/sabbaticals-solution-to-employee-burnout)

## C. The surprising / fresh angle

**The people at the center of the network are the loneliest, and AI deepens
the trap.** Gallup 2026's standout finding isn't that the disengaged are
lonely — it's that **leaders/managers score *highest* on both thriving and
loneliness simultaneously**. Pair that with the loneliness-vs-isolation
distinction: you can be maximally connected by org-chart and message volume
and still relationally starving. The agentic twist makes it sharp: an agent
gives you the *sensation* of collaboration — it answers instantly, never
pushes back socially, never needs anything from you — which is exactly the
profile of an interaction that feels social but delivers **zero of the
trust-building, error-catching, or restorative value** of a real colleague.
The fresh claim: **AI is a loneliness accelerant disguised as a
collaborator.** It removes the last functional reasons to talk to a human
right as the data says human trust is the only thing that catches what the
agent gets wrong.

---

**Connective tissue (the spine):** As agents absorb the typing and the
"doing," the scarce, load-bearing input shifts from individual output to the
relational capital between people — and that capital is built off the
keyboard, in rest and unhurried human time, not at it. Trust
(Aristotle/Lencioni/Covey) is the substrate that lets a team catch a
confident-but-wrong agent and give each other the honest "this is bad" — but
trust is drawn down faster than it's replenished when everyone ships solo
alongside a bot that never asks for help. Loneliness research shows the
danger isn't being alone (agents keep you "company"); it's relational
deficiency, which AI can mask indefinitely. And recovery science says the
repair isn't more solo focus time — it's *social* rest. So "take time away to
enjoy other humans" isn't a wellness nicety; in the agentic era it's the
maintenance schedule for the one part of the system AI can't run.
